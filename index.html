Sean Metzger

About me:

I'm a first year Ph.D. student in the UC Berkeley/UCSF graduate program in bioengineering advised by Edward Chang .

I am interested in modeling and understanding large neural population dynamics during speech, building a brain computer interface to restore speech, neural engineering, and deep learning. Before starting my Ph.D. I received a B.S. and M.S. in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University, where I did research with Daniel Palanker on retinal prostheses and Bill Dally on high efficiency power conversion.
