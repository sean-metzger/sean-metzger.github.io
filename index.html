Sean Metzger

Currently, I am a second year Ph.D. student in the UC Berkeley/UCSF graduate program in bioengineering advised by Edward Chang.

I am interested in modeling and understanding complex systems using cutting edge machine learning techniques, as well as deep unsupervised learning. Currently I model, decode, and analyze neural population activity in cortex during speech.

I am also extremely interested in sustainability, and did my B.S in EE at Stanford with a focus on green electronics, where I was fortunate to take courses in energy storage integration, device physics, and more. Before starting my Ph.D. I also obtained an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford University, where I did research with Daniel Palanker on photovoltaic retinal prosthetics, Bill Dally on high efficiency power converters for solar cells, and Mark Cappelli on plasma physics.

Previously, I was a TomKat center for Sustainable Energy summer fellow at Healyx Labs, where I helped develop a solar charging system for negative pressure wound therapy. Furthermore, I recently compeleted a freelance data-science consulting project with a leading renewable energy finance firm.
