Sean Metzger

I am a second year Ph.D. student in the UC Berkeley/UCSF graduate program in bioengineering advised by Edward Chang.

.

I am interested in modeling and understanding complex systems (eg neural activity during speech) using cutting edge machine learning techniques, as well as deep unsupervised learning, and deep learning in general. I am also extremely interested in sustainability. Before starting my Ph.D. I obtained a B.S. and M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford University, where I did research with Daniel Palanker on photovoltaic retinal prosthetics and Bill Dally on high efficiency power converters for solar cells.

I was also an TomKat center for Sustainable Energy summer fellow at Healyx Labs, where I helped develop a solar charging system for negative pressure wound therapy.
